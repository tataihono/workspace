# Ev Church Brand Guidelines

Extracted from the official Auckland Evangelical Church Brand Guidelines v2 (May 2025).

---

## 1. Brand Platform

### Persona
> We are a bunch of people, convinced we're not perfect, captivated by the historical Jesus, excited about the future he offers, and eager to authentically share this hope with Auckland, New Zealand, and the world.

### Personality
**Bold, Authentic, Warm**

### Audience
The people of Auckland, New Zealand, and abroad. Four key audience segments:

1. **Ev Church Member** — wants to easily navigate to RSVP, access resources, and share with friends
2. **Dechurched Person** — experiencing disconnection, seeking a welcoming church with vibrant community and applicable Bible teaching
3. **Unchurched Person** — has questions about Christianity, wants resources and someone to guide them toward meaning, peace, and security
4. **Other Christians** — seeks opportunities to deepen faith and connect with the wider Christian community

### Distinctives
- Captivated by Christ
- Grounded in the Gospel
- Growing in Maturity & Number

### Church Life Pathway
`Awareness → First Contact → Newish Connect → Connect Group → Committed Member → Serving Team`

### Mission
Under God, it is our aim to see the people of Auckland: Captivated by Christ; Grounded in the gospel; Growing in maturity and number.

### Vision
Under God, we want to be a church that is passionately and prayerfully committed to: Magnifying the glory of God; Connecting authentically with the world and the church; Sharing the good news of Jesus with the lost; Maturing each person in our love and knowledge of God; Equipping the next generation of Gospel workers; Planting a network of grounded and growing churches throughout Auckland and New Zealand.

## 2. Verbal Identity

### Brand Voice
**Inspiring, Inviting, Accessible**

#### Inspiring
Enthusiastic and hope-filled, pointing people to the joy we have in Christ. Inspiring gospel-grounded conviction that moves people to action.

| Feels Like | Doesn't Feel Like |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Enthusiastic | Dry |
| Compelling | Pushy |
| Purposeful | Vague |
| Substantial | Fluffy |

**Example:**
> "Come along to Recharge and be captured by God's word and hear how God has been working in the stories of our lives instead of drifting with the world around us."

**Not:**
> "Recharge is going to be epic and you'll get to hear some awesome stories from other men!"

#### Inviting
Welcoming and warm, drawing people in to be a part of our mission and vision. Creating a sense of family where everyone feels that they belong and are valued.

| Feels Like | Doesn't Feel Like |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Warm | Distant |
| Friendly | Formal |
| Genuine | Judgemental |

**Example:**
> "Join us for a night of prayer and worship as we encourage one another and point to the hope we have in the gospel."

**Not:**
> "We'll be having a prayer and worship night to reflect on the hope of the gospel."

#### Accessible
Clear and conversational, using everyday language that's easy to follow, without watering down truth. Understandable and relatable to everyone, regardless of spiritual or cultural background.

| Feels Like | Doesn't Feel Like |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Clear | Wordy |
| Conversational | Jargon-heavy |
| Inclusive | Unrelatable |

**Example:**
> "Come and hear what the Bible has to say about the spiritual realm and how we should think about spiritual things."

**Not:**
> "Come and hear a doctrinal exploration angelology, demonology, and the cosmic implications of spiritual warfare."

## 4. Logo System

### Primary Logo
- Square `ev.church` lockup in rounded square container
- **Must always use the official rounded square container**
- The stacked "ev.church" text may never be used standalone
- "ev." may never be used without "church"

### Secondary Logo
- Single-line `ev.church`
- Use where the square version doesn't fit or where a longer version complements the design
- Use a container version if background is busy
- The "ev." element may be used as a graphic element where the curve of the "C" hangs over the edge — but the full logo must be visible elsewhere on that design

### Tertiary Logo
- Single-line `ev.church` + "Auckland Evangelical Church"
- For legal documentation or grant applications
- Container versions (white/black) available for busy backgrounds

### Logo Clearance
- **Primary (square):** Space on all sides equal to at least 1/3 of logo height
- **Secondary/Tertiary:** Space on all sides equal to at least the width of the letter "H" as it appears in the logo

### Minimum Sizes
| Logo | Min Height (Print) | Min Height (Digital) |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Primary (square) | 2.5 cm | 70.87 px (72ppi) |
| Secondary (single-line) | 1 cm | 25.38 px |
| Tertiary (with full name) | 2 cm | 56.69 px |
| Full lockup | 3 cm | 85.04 px |

### Logo Don'ts
- Do not change any colours in the logo
- Do not use low-contrast backgrounds
- Do not lower transparency — keep 100% opacity
- Do not rotate the logo or any parts
- Do not use drop shadow, bevel, emboss, or any effects
- Do not stretch, squash, or warp disproportionally
- These rules apply to all logos within the Ev brand

## 6. Typography

### Primary Typeface: Proxima Nova
- **Type:** Sans-serif
- **Character:** Clear, bold, friendly
- **Source:** Adobe Fonts (TypeKit)
- **Available weights:** Thin, Regular, Bold, Black (+ italic variants)
- **Usage:** Headings, subheadings, and body copy
- **Restriction:** Do not use condensed versions
- When used as the only font, use clear visual hierarchy (weight, size, spacing) to differentiate heading levels

### Secondary Typeface: Utopia
- **Type:** Serif
- **Character:** Warm, elegant, versatile
- **Source:** Adobe Fonts
- **Available weights:** Thin, Regular (avoid heavier than semi-bold)
- **Usage:** Headings, subheadings, and body copy — but should appear less than Proxima Nova overall
- **Web fallback:** Georgia Pro

### Hierarchy Rules
- Subheadings should be **no larger than 50%** of the headline's ascender height
- Body copy should be **no larger than 50%** of the subheading's cap height

### Reference Sizes (from brand guide examples)
| Level | Typeface | Weight | Size |
|-------|----------|--------|------|
| Headline | Georgia Pro (Utopia alt) | Regular | 75pt |
| Subheading | Proxima Nova | Regular, often ALL CAPS, tracked | 41pt |
| Body | Proxima Nova | Regular | 20pt |

### Line Height
- Calculate by multiplying font size by 1.1 to 1.6
- **As type size increases, decrease the line height multiplier**
- Reference values from brand guide:
  - 42pt → line-height multiplier 1.1
  - 30pt → line-height multiplier 1.3
  - 18pt → line-height multiplier 1.4

### Typography Combinations (from brand guide)

| Combination | Heading | Subheading | Body |
|-------------|---------|------------|------|
| **A** | Proxima Nova Bold | Georgia Pro Italic | Proxima Nova Regular |
| **B** | Proxima Nova Light | Georgia Pro Italic | Georgia Pro Regular |
| **C** | Proxima Nova ExtraBold | Proxima Nova Bold ALL CAPS | Georgia Pro Regular |
| **D** | Georgia Pro Regular | Proxima Nova Regular ALL CAPS | Proxima Nova Regular |

## 7. Photography

### Style
- **Warm, authentic, inspiring**
- Natural and spontaneous — capture real interactions
- Avoid photography that feels contrived or staged
- Use a variety of shots to capture different moments

### Shot Types
1. Candid photos
2. Posed photos (smiles, looking into camera)
3. Kids and youth photos
4. Environment photos
5. Serving photos
6. Crowd photos

### Visual & Emotional Characteristics
- **Focal point:** One clear focal point per image. No visual clutter.
- **Colour:** Natural, vibrant but not oversaturated
- **Depth of field:** Shallow — draw viewer to subject
- **Lighting:** Natural light as much as possible
- **Emotional feel:** Fun, exciting, genuine, inspiring, heartwarming
- **Engagement:** Viewer should feel like a participant, not just an observer

### Photo Don'ts
- Too much visual clutter
- No clear subject or focal point
- Shot in JPEG instead of RAW
- Awkward framing
- No emotional connection
- Faces of main subjects hidden or too small
- Face masks (dates the photo)
- Not enough headroom (too close to top edge)

### Practical Tips
1. Always shoot in RAW
2. Consider consent — would the person be okay with this posted online?
3. Capture before/during/after service moments
4. Avoid flash, turn off camera noises, be mindful of positioning
5. Use transition points (e.g., during prayer) to move around
6. Include negative space for text/design overlays
7. Quality over quantity — don't overshoot
