# Comprehensive Content Review: ev.church

A strategic review of ev.church content through the lens of the church's three mission pillars and SEO effectiveness for reaching Auckland.

---

## Executive Summary

Ev Church's website effectively communicates its identity as a Reformed Evangelical church, but **undersells its heart**. The content is structurally sound but reads more like an organizational brochure than an invitation into a living, Christ-centered community. The site's biggest opportunity is moving from *informing* visitors to *compelling* them — from "here's what we do" to "here's what God is doing, and you're invited."

The SEO foundation is weak. There is minimal structured data, thin meta descriptions, no blog/content strategy, and the site relies almost entirely on brand-name searches rather than reaching people in the moments they're actually seeking God, community, or answers.

**The bottom line:** The content is theologically faithful but strategically underdeveloped. With targeted improvements, this site could become a genuine evangelistic tool for reaching Auckland's 120,000 — not just a digital bulletin board for existing members.

## Part 1: Mission Alignment Review

### Pillar 1: Captivated by Christ

**What's working:**
- The About page clearly articulates Reformed theology with a Christ-centered confession
- "Services at Ev Church are all about Jesus" appears multiple times — a strong, clear statement
- The current sermon series ("Jesus - Light & Life" in John) is front and center on the homepage
- The Easter page is genuinely excellent — the three-day narrative is compelling, theologically rich, and emotionally resonant

**What's missing:**
- **Christ is talked about but not encountered on the site.** The content describes what the church believes about Jesus but rarely lets visitors hear Jesus's own words, see transformed lives, or feel the weight of the gospel. The site tells; it rarely shows.
- **No testimonies or stories.** Across all 17 pages, there is not a single personal story of someone encountering Christ through Ev Church. For a church of 700+ members, this is a significant gap. Stories are how people see themselves in the gospel.
- **The sermon content is a dead end.** The homepage shows the current series but links to an external resources site (resources.aucklandev.co.nz). There's no embedded player, no sermon archive on the main site, no way for a curious visitor to hear a message before deciding to attend. This is a major missed opportunity — the sermon is the primary way someone can experience "captivated by Christ" before stepping through the door.
- **Scripture is underused.** Outside the Easter page (which uses it beautifully — John 11:25, Isaiah 53:5, Matthew 28:6), the rest of the site rarely quotes the Bible directly. The Vision page references 1 Corinthians 1:23-24 and Jonah, but most pages are written in the church's voice, not God's voice. For a church grounded in "the Bible as supreme authority," the website should let Scripture speak more.
- **The Welcome section is generic.** "We're one church gathered across three campuses, unified as people who are captivated by Jesus, grounded in the gospel, and growing in maturity and number" — this is mission-statement language, not warm hospitality language. A first-time visitor doesn't know what "grounded in the gospel" means. They want to know: *Will I be welcomed? Will this make sense to me? Will I find something real here?*

**Recommendations:**
1. **Add a "Stories" or "Lives Changed" section** to the homepage — 2-3 short video or written testimonies of real people. A new believer from Explaining Christianity. A family who found community. A student at Unichurch who was skeptical.
2. **Embed sermon content directly.** Add a sermon player on the homepage and create a `/sermons` page. Let people listen before they visit.
3. **Use Scripture as section headers and pull quotes** throughout the site. Not just as decoration — as the actual content that shapes each page. Example: the Visit page could open with "Come to me, all who are weary..." (Matt 11:28).
4. **Rewrite the Welcome section** in warmer, more human language. Address the visitor directly: "Whether you're exploring faith for the first time, returning after years away, or looking for a church to call home — you're welcome here."

### Pillar 2: Grounded in the Gospel

**What's working:**
- The "What We Believe" section is doctrinally thorough and faithfully Reformed — Trinity, substitutionary atonement, grace alone through faith alone, the authority of Scripture
- Explaining Christianity is well-positioned as a low-barrier entry point — "relaxed setting," "good food," "friendly, genuine conversations"
- The Easter FAQ does an outstanding job of presenting the gospel to non-Christians (Why did Jesus die? What does Easter mean for me? Can I come if I'm not a Christian?)

**What's missing:**
- **The gospel is assumed, not presented, on most pages.** Someone landing on the homepage, Visit page, or any campus page would learn that Ev Church exists, where it meets, and what programs it offers — but they would not hear the gospel. The site presumes familiarity with Christianity. This is a critical gap for a church aiming to reach 120,000 unchurched Aucklanders.
- **"Explaining Christianity" is buried in a sub-menu.** This is arguably the most evangelistically important page on the site, yet it's hidden under "Next Steps" — a label that assumes someone has already taken a first step. A seeker Googling "what is Christianity" or "explore faith Auckland" would never find this page. It needs to be far more prominent and discoverable.
- **The pathway from curious → exploring → belonging is unclear.** The site has the pieces — Explaining Christianity → Newish Connect → Connect Groups — but this journey is never explicitly mapped out for the visitor. Each page exists in isolation. A visitor on the Explaining Christianity page doesn't know what comes after. A visitor on Connect Groups doesn't know Explaining Christianity exists for their non-Christian friend.
- **The beliefs content, while thorough, is inaccessible to outsiders.** The "What We Believe" section uses theological language ("substitutionary death," "regeneration," "illumination") that is meaningful to believers but opaque to seekers. This is an internal document presented externally. Consider a parallel version: "What Does This Mean For You?" that translates each belief into plain language and personal relevance.
- **No gospel presentation exists as a standalone page.** The Easter page comes closest, but it's seasonal. There should be an evergreen page — something like "/the-gospel" or "/who-is-jesus" — that clearly presents the Christian message for someone who has never heard it. This page would be a primary SEO target and the destination for social media evangelism links.

**Recommendations:**
1. **Create a "/gospel" or "/who-is-jesus" page** — a clear, warm, beautiful presentation of the gospel message. This becomes a primary landing page for outreach and SEO.
2. **Elevate Explaining Christianity** to primary navigation. Rename the "Next Steps" dropdown to something seekers understand: "Explore" or "Start Here." Make Explaining Christianity the first item.
3. **Build an explicit journey pathway** — a visual on the Visit page or a dedicated page showing: *Curious → Explaining Christianity → Sunday Service → Newish Connect → Connect Group → Serving*. Show people the on-ramp.
4. **Add a "plain language" layer to beliefs** — keep the theological depth but add a sentence under each heading that says what it means in everyday terms. "We believe in the Trinity" → "God is one God in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — eternally equal in power and love."

### Pillar 3: Growing in Maturity and Number

**What's working:**
- The Vision 2030 page is bold and inspiring — 120,000 goal, 2,030 members, 6 campuses, church planting
- The three-campus model with a dedicated Unichurch for university students shows strategic thinking about growth
- Connect Groups are positioned well as the discipleship mechanism
- Youth and Kids programs show investment in the next generation

**What's missing:**
- **Growth is aspirational but not evidenced.** The Vision page states the 120,000 goal and the history (8 people → 700+), but the homepage and campus pages don't show a growing, vibrant community. There are no attendance indicators, no "X people baptised this year," no growth stories. For a church declaring bold growth goals, the site should demonstrate that God is already at work.
- **Maturity pathways are incomplete.** Connect Groups are described briefly, but the site offers no depth: What books are you studying? What does spiritual growth look like at Ev? How do I go from attending to leading? There's no discipleship content, no recommended reading, no equipping resources beyond the external resources site.
- **The "Number" side needs on-ramps, not just programs.** Every page should make it easy for someone to take a next step. Currently, most pages end with "New Here?" pointing to /visit. But a visitor who has already attended needs a different next step. The site doesn't distinguish between: never attended, attended once, attending regularly, ready to commit, ready to serve, ready to lead.
- **Campus pages are underutilized for growth.** Each campus page shows events but no campus-specific personality, no campus pastor's welcome, no photos of actual people, no sense of what makes North different from Central or Unichurch. These pages should make someone feel like they already belong before they walk in.
- **Connect Groups page is the thinnest page on the site.** For a church that names connect groups as its primary discipleship vehicle, this page has exactly two paragraphs and a redirect to Newish Connect. It should be one of the richest pages on the site — showcasing available groups, their meeting times, leaders, what they're studying, and how to join.
- **No equipping/training content.** The Vision mentions "establish a gospel training hub" and "train and send 30 people into full-time ministry" — but the website has no training content, no pathways to ministry, no information about apprenticeships (despite having 5 apprentices on staff).

**Recommendations:**
1. **Add social proof to the homepage** — "Join 700+ people across Auckland who gather each week" or similar. Show baptism numbers, growth trajectory, or people served.
2. **Build out the Connect Groups page** with actual group listings: day, time, location, leader, current study, "Join" button. Use the data from Rock RMS Groups that the Lava templates already support.
3. **Create a "/serve" or "/get-involved" page** — show ministry areas, volunteer needs, and how to take the step from attender to participant.
4. **Create a "/training" or "/equip" page** — showcase the apprenticeship program, ministry training opportunities, and how the church develops leaders. This supports the Vision 2030 goal and could attract purpose-driven Christians looking for a church that takes discipleship seriously.
5. **Give each campus page a distinct voice** — a welcome message from the campus lead, photos of real gatherings, and a "Here's what's unique about us" section.

## Part 2: SEO Strategy Review

### Current State Assessment

The current site has **critical SEO gaps** that severely limit its ability to reach unchurched Aucklanders through organic search.

#### Technical SEO Issues

| Issue | Severity | Details |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Missing meta descriptions | High | Most pages lack custom meta descriptions. Google will auto-generate snippets, often poorly. |
| No structured data | High | Zero JSON-LD markup across the entire site. No Organization, LocalBusiness, Event, Church, FAQ, or Person schema. |
| No sitemap.xml | Medium | The Rock CMS site doesn't appear to generate an XML sitemap. |
| Missing Open Graph tags | Medium | Social sharing previews are absent or generic. |
| No canonical tags | Medium | Duplicate content risk between www and non-www, http and https. |
| Image alt text missing | Medium | Carousel images, campus photos, and team photos lack alt text. |
| Title tags are generic | Medium | Page titles don't include location-qualified keywords. |
| No hreflang | Low | Single-language site, but NZ-specific content should signal geo-targeting. |
| External resources on subdomain | Medium | Sermon content on resources.aucklandev.co.nz doesn't benefit the main domain's authority. |

#### Content SEO Issues

| Issue | Impact | Details |
|-------|--------|---------|
| No blog or regular content | Critical | The site publishes no indexable content between Sundays. Google rewards fresh, regular content. A church publishing weekly sermon summaries, devotionals, or articles would dramatically outperform a static site. |
| Thin pages | High | Connect Groups (2 paragraphs), Explaining Christianity (1 paragraph), Newish Connect (1 paragraph), Give (redirect) — these pages offer Google almost nothing to index. |
| No location pages beyond campuses | High | No content targeting suburb-level searches ("church Rosedale," "church Hillsborough," "church near University of Auckland"). |
| No topical content | High | No pages targeting questions people actually search: "what is the gospel," "churches near me Auckland," "is Christianity true," "how to start going to church." |
| Keyword cannibalization | Medium | Multiple pages compete for "church Auckland" without distinct targeting. |

### Structured Data Recommendations

Implement these JSON-LD schemas across the site:

```
Homepage:        Organization + WebSite + SearchAction
Campus pages:    LocalBusiness (Church subtype) with geo, hours, images
About page:      AboutPage + Person (Senior Pastor)
Events:          Event schema for each upcoming event
Easter:          Event (per service) + FAQPage
Kids/Youth:      Organization with department
Visit:           FAQPage (what to expect questions)
Sermon Series:   PodcastSeries or CreativeWork
Contact:         ContactPage with address
```

**Estimated impact:** Structured data can increase click-through rates by 20-30% through rich snippets (event dates, FAQ dropdowns, star ratings, location info in search results).

### Content Strategy: The Missing Piece

**The single biggest SEO opportunity is a content strategy.** The current site is static — it only changes when events update. Google rewards sites that regularly publish fresh, relevant, indexable content.

#### Recommended Content Types

1. **Weekly Sermon Summary / Blog Post** (1 per week)
   - 500-800 words summarizing the Sunday message
   - Target keyword: the passage or topic (e.g., "John 3:16 meaning," "gospel of John study")
   - Include audio embed, key quotes, discussion questions
   - This alone would generate 52 new indexed pages per year

2. **Devotional / Article Series** (2-4 per month)
   - Short articles on faith, life, and culture from a gospel perspective
   - Target exploratory keywords: "how to pray," "dealing with anxiety as a Christian," "what does the Bible say about..."
   - Written by pastors and staff
   - Positions the church as a thought leader and resource

3. **Seasonal Landing Pages** (4-6 per year)
   - Easter, Christmas, Back to Church, special events
   - Target high-volume seasonal keywords
   - Reusable templates in Payload CMS

4. **Neighbourhood / Community Pages** (3-5 evergreen pages)
   - "Church in Rosedale," "Church on the North Shore," "Church near University of Auckland"
   - Local SEO targeting with community-specific content
   - Include directions, nearby landmarks, parking info

5. **Gospel / Evangelistic Pages** (3-5 evergreen pages)
   - "/the-gospel" — clear gospel presentation
   - "/who-is-jesus" — accessible introduction to Jesus
   - "/why-church" — addressing common objections
   - Target high-volume exploratory queries
   - These are the digital equivalent of Explaining Christianity

## Part 4: Specific Content Rewrites

### Homepage Welcome Section — Current vs Recommended

**Current:**
> "Welcome to Ev Church. We're one church gathered across three campuses, unified as people who are captivated by Jesus, grounded in the gospel, and growing in maturity and number."

**Recommended:**
> "Welcome to Ev Church. Whether you're exploring faith for the first time, looking for a church to call home, or deepening your walk with Jesus — there's a place for you here. We're a community of Christ-followers scattered across Auckland's North Shore, Central, and the University, gathering each Sunday because we've found that life with Jesus changes everything. Come and see."

*Why:* The current version is mission-statement language directed inward. The rewrite addresses the visitor directly, acknowledges different starting points (seeker, church-shopper, growing Christian), and issues a warm invitation. It's still theologically grounded but speaks human first.

---

### Explaining Christianity — Current vs Recommended

**Current:**
> "Explaining Christianity is a relaxed setting to investigate the message and claims behind Christianity. Four weeks of good food, engaging talks about Jesus and the Bible, and opportunities for discussion and questions."

**Recommended:**
> "Got questions about God, life, or what Christians actually believe? You're not alone — and you're welcome here.
>
> Explaining Christianity is a free, four-week experience where you can explore the Christian faith in an honest, no-pressure environment. Each evening includes a meal together, a short talk exploring a big question from the Bible, and open discussion where every question is welcome — from 'Is God real?' to 'Why does suffering exist?'
>
> You don't need to believe anything to come. You don't need to know anyone. You just need to be curious.
>
> **What you'll explore:**
> - Week 1: Who is Jesus and why does he matter?
> - Week 2: What's the Bible actually about?
> - Week 3: Why did Jesus die — and does it affect me?
> - Week 4: What does it mean to follow Jesus today?
>
> **Upcoming sessions:** [dates, locations]
>
> *'I came as a skeptic and left with something I didn't expect — hope.'* — Sarah, 2025 participant"

*Why:* The original is 2 sentences for the church's most important evangelistic tool. The rewrite: (a) leads with the visitor's questions, not the program's description; (b) removes fear ("no-pressure," "you don't need to believe anything"); (c) shows what each week covers; (d) includes social proof; (e) is 10x more indexable by Google for queries like "explore Christianity Auckland" or "free Christianity course."

### Campus Pages — Missing Elements

Each campus page should include (currently absent from all three):

1. **Welcome from the campus lead** — 2-3 sentences + photo. Makes it personal.
2. **"What our Sunday looks like"** — timeline graphic: 10:00 Doors open, 10:15 Worship, 10:30 Kids dismissed, 10:40 Bible talk, 11:15 Closing, 11:30 Coffee & connection
3. **Community photos** — Real photos of real people (not just building/stage shots). Show diversity, warmth, laughter, worship.
4. **Parking & access info** — Practical details that remove friction.
5. **Campus-specific connect groups** — "Meet midweek in the Rosedale area" with group options.
6. **A distinct sentence of identity:**
   - North: "A gospel-shaped community loving the North Shore with truth and compassion"
   - Central: "Where Ev Church began — proclaiming Christ in the heart of Auckland since 2012"
   - Unichurch: "Making Jesus known on campus and beyond — a church for students and young adults"

## Part 5: Quick Wins (Implementable in the Next.js Rebuild)

These can be built into the Next.js + Payload CMS rebuild from day one:

1. **Meta tags infrastructure** — Every page in Payload CMS should have `metaTitle`, `metaDescription`, and `ogImage` fields. Set character count guidance (50-60 for title, 150-160 for description).

2. **JSON-LD component** — Build a reusable `<StructuredData>` component that generates Organization, LocalBusiness, Event, and FAQPage schema from Payload data. Wire it into the layout.

3. **Sitemap generation** — Next.js App Router supports `sitemap.ts`. Auto-generate from Payload pages + campuses + events.

4. **Image optimization** — Next.js `<Image>` component with automatic WebP/AVIF conversion, responsive srcset, lazy loading, and blur placeholder. Enforce alt text as required field in Payload.

5. **Canonical URLs** — Set automatically via `<link rel="canonical">` on every page.

6. **Internal linking** — Build a "Related Pages" or "Next Steps" component that appears at the bottom of every page, guiding visitors deeper.

7. **Analytics events** — Track: form submissions, "Plan Your Visit" clicks, sermon plays, campus card clicks, CTA button clicks. Measure the path from homepage → visit page → form submission.

8. **A/B testable headlines** — Use Payload CMS draft/preview functionality to test different homepage headlines and CTAs against conversion metrics.
