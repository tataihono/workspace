## The Easter Story - Three Days

### Good Friday: "The Ultimate Cost"

True peace came at a cost. Jesus willingly sacrificed himself so that we could be reconciled to God. His death was not an accident or a defeat — it was the plan all along.

**Scripture:** "But he was pierced because of our rebellion, crushed because of our iniquities; punishment for our peace was on him, and we are healed by his wounds." — Isaiah 53:5 CSB

### Saturday: "The Silent Saturday"

A time of grief and waiting. The disciples mourned, uncertain of what would come next. Yet even in the silence, God was still working. The story was not over.

### Easter Sunday: "Dawn of Hope"

Death lost its grip. Jesus rose from the grave, defeating death and offering forgiveness and eternal life to all who place their faith in him.

**Scripture:** "He is not here. For he has risen, just as he said." — Matthew 28:6 CSB

## FAQ Section

### What actually happened at Easter?

Easter remembers the most significant events in Christian history. On Good Friday, Jesus was crucified and died. He was buried in a tomb. On the third day — Easter Sunday — he rose from the dead. Christians believe these events are the foundation of their faith: that Jesus' death paid the price for sin and his resurrection proved he was who he claimed to be.

### Why did Jesus have to die?

The Bible teaches that all people have sinned and are separated from God. The consequence of sin is death. Jesus, who lived a perfect life, willingly took the punishment we deserved. His death was a substitutionary sacrifice — he died in our place so that we could be forgiven and restored to relationship with God.

### Did the resurrection really happen?

Christians believe the resurrection is a historical event, not just a spiritual metaphor. The Bible documents multiple eyewitness accounts of people who saw Jesus alive after his death — over 500 people at one point. The disciples, who had fled in fear at his arrest, were transformed into bold witnesses willing to die for what they had seen. The historical evidence and the biblical documentation point to a real, bodily resurrection.

### What does Easter mean for me personally?

Easter means that forgiveness and new life are available to everyone. It is not something you earn through good behavior — it is a gift of grace. The Bible says that anyone who puts their trust in Jesus receives forgiveness for their sins and the promise of eternal life. Easter is an invitation to accept that gift.

### I'm not a Christian. Can I still come?

Absolutely. You are more than welcome. Our Easter services are designed for everyone — whether you have been following Jesus for years or are just curious about what Christians believe. There is no pressure and no expectations. Come as you are, ask your questions, and explore at your own pace. We would love to have you.

### What happens at an Easter service?

Our Easter services run for about 75 minutes. You can expect singing (a mix of contemporary worship music), a teaching from the Bible about the meaning of Easter, and a time of prayer. There are age-appropriate kids' programs running during the morning services so your children are cared for and engaged. After the service, stick around for barista coffee and a chance to meet people.
