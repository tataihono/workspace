About Ev Church

Our Mission

Ev Church exists to magnify God's glory, connect authentically with both the world and the church, share the good news of Jesus with the lost, and equip gospel workers to plant churches throughout Auckland and New Zealand.

Ev Church is an evangelical and independent church, upholding the historic creeds and Reformation theology centered on grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone.

Who We Are

We are a community of Christ-followers committed to the gospel, gathered across multiple campuses in Auckland, New Zealand. We hold to the historic Christian faith as expressed in the creeds of the early church and recovered in the Reformation.

What We Believe

God

We believe in one eternal God who exists as three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Each person of the Trinity is fully God, co-equal and co-eternal, united in purpose and nature.

Humanity

We believe that all people are created in the image of God, with inherent dignity and worth. However, through the fall of Adam, all humanity is universally sinful and separated from God, unable to save themselves.

Bible

We believe the Bible is divinely inspired by God and stands as the supreme authority for all matters of faith and practice. It is trustworthy, sufficient, and the final word on truth.

Jesus Christ

We believe that Jesus Christ is fully God and fully human. He was born of a virgin, lived a sinless life, died on the cross for our sins, rose bodily from the dead, ascended into heaven, and will return again in glory.

Salvation

We believe salvation comes through Christ's substitutionary death on the cross and is received by God's grace alone through faith alone. It cannot be earned by human effort or merit.

Holy Spirit

We believe the Holy Spirit illuminates believers, convicts of sin, regenerates the heart, empowers for godly living, and builds up the church through spiritual gifts.

Church

We believe the church is the gathered community of believers, called to witness to God's rule and reign, to make disciples of all nations, and to love and serve one another and the world.
